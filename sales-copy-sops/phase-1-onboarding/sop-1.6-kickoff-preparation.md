# SOP 1.6: Kick-off Call Preparation

## Purpose
Prepare everything needed for a productive kick-off call with the client, including a comprehensive question bank, call structure, and documentation system.

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### Step 1: Schedule the Call
**[TIME: 5 minutes]**

Send scheduling request to client:

```
Subject: [Project Name] Kick-off Call Scheduling

Hi [Client Name],

I've reviewed the materials you sent and I'm excited to dive deeper into [Product Name].

I'd like to schedule our kick-off call to discuss:
- Your ideal customer in detail
- The story behind [product/company]
- What makes your approach unique
- Any additional insights that will help me write powerful copy

This call typically runs 60-90 minutes. Please let me know your availability for the next [timeframe].

Looking forward to it!
[Your Name]

P.S. If you have any additional testimonials, case studies, or customer feedback to share before our call, please send them my way.
```

**Call logistics:**
- [ ] Duration: 60-90 minutes (block 2 hours to be safe)
- [ ] Platform: [Zoom/Google Meet/Phone]
- [ ] Recording permission: Get consent before recording
- [ ] Calendar invite sent with agenda

### Step 2: Prepare Question Bank
**[TIME: 30-45 minutes]**

Organize questions into categories. Customize based on product type and gaps identified.

---

## Master Question Bank

### SECTION 1: CUSTOMER DEEP-DIVE

#### 1.1 Customer Profile
- "Describe your ideal customer. Not just demographics, but who they really are."
- "If I walked into a room full of your best customers, what would I notice about them?"
- "What's the #1 thing that makes someone a perfect fit for this?"
- "Who is this NOT for? Who should definitely not buy this?"
- "What's the average age/background/situation of your customers?"

#### 1.2 Pain Points & Problems
- "What's the main problem people are trying to solve when they come to you?"
- "When someone reaches out to you, what do they typically say is wrong?"
- "What are they frustrated about? What have they tried that didn't work?"
- "What's the pain they deal with on a daily basis because of this problem?"
- "If you had to describe their 'rock bottom' moment, what would that look like?"
- "What exact words or phrases do your customers use to describe their problem?"
- "What do they complain about the most?"
- "What keeps them up at night related to this?"

#### 1.3 Desires & Dream Outcomes
- "When someone achieves success with your product, what does that look like?"
- "What's the dream outcome your customers are hoping for?"
- "Beyond the obvious result, what does achieving this mean for their life?"
- "What would they be able to do that they can't do now?"
- "How would they feel differently?"
- "What would change in their relationships/career/health/life?"
- "What exact words do customers use when they describe what they want?"

#### 1.4 Objections & Hesitations
- "What's the #1 reason someone doesn't buy?"
- "What objections do you hear most often on sales calls?"
- "What do skeptical people say about your product/approach?"
- "What makes people hesitate even when they're interested?"
- "What's the biggest misconception people have about this?"
- "What questions do people ask before they buy?"

#### 1.5 Trigger Events
- "What typically triggers someone to start looking for a solution?"
- "What's usually happening in their life when they find you?"
- "Is there a specific moment or event that makes them say 'enough is enough'?"
- "What season/time of year do you see most interest?"

---

### SECTION 2: FAILED SOLUTIONS & ALTERNATIVES

#### 2.1 What They've Tried
- "What have your customers typically tried before finding you?"
- "What solutions are they coming from?"
- "What did those solutions promise? Why did they fail?"
- "What do you hear people say about [specific competitor]?"
- "What's the 'common wisdom' approach to this problem? Does it work?"

#### 2.2 Why Other Things Don't Work
- "Why don't [common solutions] work for most people?"
- "What do other solutions get wrong?"
- "What's the fundamental problem with the traditional approach?"
- "What are people missing when they try to solve this themselves?"

---

### SECTION 3: UNIQUE MECHANISM & DIFFERENTIATION

#### 3.1 What Makes You Different
- "What makes your approach different from everything else out there?"
- "If you had to explain why YOUR solution works when others don't, what would you say?"
- "What's the core insight or principle that everything else is built on?"
- "Do you have a name for your methodology/system/approach?"
- "What's the counter-intuitive thing you've discovered that others miss?"
- "If there's ONE thing that makes this work, what is it?"

#### 3.2 How It Works
- "Walk me through how this actually works for someone."
- "What's the process? What are the key steps or phases?"
- "Why does doing it THIS way produce results?"
- "Is there a visual way to explain how this works?"
- "What's the science/logic behind why this works?"

#### 3.3 The "Aha" Moment
- "What's the 'aha' moment your customers have?"
- "When does the light bulb go on for them?"
- "What do customers say when they finally 'get it'?"

---

### SECTION 4: PROOF & RESULTS

#### 4.1 Customer Results
- "What's the best result a customer has achieved?"
- "What's a typical result? What can most people expect?"
- "How long does it typically take to see results?"
- "Do you have specific numbers/metrics from customer success stories?"
- "Can you tell me about 3-5 customers with great stories?"

#### 4.2 Unique Proof
- "What proof do you have that no competitor can claim?"
- "Is there data, research, or statistics that support your approach?"
- "Have you been featured in any media or publications?"
- "Any endorsements from respected figures in the industry?"

#### 4.3 Credentials
- "What's your background? Why should people listen to you on this?"
- "What credentials or experience make you qualified to offer this?"
- "How long have you been doing this?"
- "How many people have you helped?"

---

### SECTION 5: STORY & ORIGIN

#### 5.1 Founder/Creator Story
- "How did you get into this? What's your story?"
- "Why do you do this? What's your personal connection to this problem?"
- "Was there a moment that changed everything for you?"
- "Did you struggle with this problem yourself?"

#### 5.2 Product Origin Story
- "How did this product/service come to be?"
- "What was the insight or discovery that led to creating this?"
- "Did something specific happen that made you realize you needed to create this?"
- "What were you doing before? What made you shift to this?"

#### 5.3 Mission & Purpose
- "What's your bigger mission with this?"
- "If you achieved everything you wanted, what would be different in the world?"
- "Why does this matter to you beyond making money?"

---

### SECTION 6: PRODUCT SPECIFICS

#### 6.1 What's Included
- "Walk me through exactly what someone gets when they buy."
- "What are the main components/modules/elements?"
- "What bonuses or extras are included?"
- "Is there any support or community access?"
- "How is the product delivered? What's the experience like?"

#### 6.2 Why This Format
- "Why did you structure it this way?"
- "Why these specific elements and not others?"
- "What's included that people don't expect but love?"

---

### SECTION 7: MARKET & COMPETITION

#### 7.1 Competitive Landscape
- "Who do you see as your main competitors?"
- "What do competitors do well? What do they miss?"
- "How do you position yourself against them?"
- "What would someone say comparing you to [competitor]?"

#### 7.2 Market Positioning
- "Where do you fit in the market? Premium? Budget? Different?"
- "Who's the bigger player you're positioning against?"
- "What category do people put you in?"

---

### SECTION 8: OFFER & OBJECTIONS

#### 8.1 Pricing & Value
- "Why is it priced at [price]?"
- "What makes it worth this price?"
- "What would someone have to pay to get similar results elsewhere?"

#### 8.2 Guarantee
- "What's your guarantee?"
- "Why that specific guarantee?"
- "What's your refund rate?"

#### 8.3 Urgency/Scarcity
- "Is there any real scarcity or urgency? (Limited spots, price increase, bonuses expire)"
- "Why should someone act now vs. later?"

---

### SECTION 9: EXTRAS & WILD CARDS

#### 9.1 Interesting Facts
- "Is there anything surprising or counter-intuitive about this?"
- "Any interesting facts or statistics that would grab attention?"
- "Anything you wish people knew about this that they don't?"

#### 9.2 Open-Ended
- "What haven't I asked that I should have?"
- "Is there anything else you want to share that would help me write better copy?"
- "What would you tell your best friend about this if they needed it?"

---

### Step 3: Customize Question Set
**[TIME: 15-20 minutes]**

Based on product type and gaps, select and customize questions:

#### For Digital Products (Courses, SaaS, Memberships):
Prioritize:
- Customer transformation journey
- Unique methodology/framework
- Course structure and why
- Student success stories with specifics

#### For Nutritional Supplements:
Prioritize:
- Ingredient uniqueness and dosing
- Scientific backing
- Why this formulation
- Compliance-friendly result stories
- Manufacturing/quality story

#### For Service Providers (Coaches, Consultants):
Prioritize:
- Personal story and credibility
- Methodology/process
- Client transformation stories
- Why 1:1/group vs. other formats
- What they do that others don't

### Step 4: Create Call Agenda
**[TIME: 10 minutes]**

```markdown
# Kick-off Call Agenda

## Client: [Name]
## Date: [Date]
## Duration: 90 minutes

---

### Pre-Call (5 min)
- Test recording
- Confirm permission to record
- Small talk / rapport building

### Part 1: Customer Deep-Dive (20-25 min)
- Who is the ideal customer?
- Pain points and struggles
- Dream outcomes and desires
- Failed solutions they've tried

### Part 2: Product & Mechanism (20-25 min)
- What makes your approach different?
- How does it actually work?
- The unique mechanism/methodology
- Why does it work when others don't?

### Part 3: Proof & Story (15-20 min)
- Your story / origin story
- Best customer results
- Credentials and authority
- Unique proof points

### Part 4: Offer & Objections (10-15 min)
- Common objections
- Guarantee and risk reversal
- Why this price point

### Part 5: Wrap-up (5-10 min)
- Anything I should have asked?
- Next steps
- Timeline review

---

### Post-Call
- Process recording
- Update brief
- Send follow-up
```

## Call Best Practices

### Before the Call
- [ ] Review preliminary brief
- [ ] Have questions printed/open
- [ ] Test recording
- [ ] Close distracting apps
- [ ] Have water nearby

### During the Call
- [ ] Record the call (with permission)
- [ ] Take notes on key quotes
- [ ] Listen more than talk (80/20 rule)
- [ ] Ask follow-up questions
- [ ] Capture EXACT phrases
- [ ] Note emotional moments
- [ ] Don't rush to the next question

### After the Call
- [ ] Process recording within 24 hours
- [ ] Update brief with new information
- [ ] Note exact quotes to use
- [ ] Identify research priorities
- [ ] Send thank-you/follow-up

---

## Common Issues & Solutions

| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| Client gives short answers | Use follow-up probes: "Can you tell me more about that?" |
| Client goes off-topic | Gently redirect: "That's interesting - I want to come back to..." |
| Running out of time | Prioritize must-ask questions; schedule follow-up if needed |
| Client doesn't know their customer well | Shift to asking about specific customers they know |
| Recording fails | Take detailed notes; request a follow-up call if critical |
