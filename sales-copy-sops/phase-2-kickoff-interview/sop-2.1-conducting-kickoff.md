# SOP 2.1: Conducting the Kick-off Call

## Purpose
Execute a comprehensive client interview that extracts the deep insights needed to write powerful, resonant copy.

## Pre-Call Checklist (15 min before)

### Technical Setup
- [ ] Video/audio platform open and tested
- [ ] Recording software ready (get permission at start)
- [ ] Backup recording method ready (phone, secondary app)
- [ ] Good lighting and audio
- [ ] Quiet environment secured
- [ ] "Do not disturb" mode on all devices

### Documents Ready

- [ ] Reviewed brief and key gaps
- [ ] Clear on priority questions
- [ ] In curious, listening mindset

## Step-by-Step Call Procedure

### OPENING (5-7 minutes)

#### Step 1: Warm Welcome
```
"Hey [Name], great to see you! Thanks for making time for this.
How's everything going?"
```
*[2-3 minutes of rapport building - genuinely connect]*

#### Step 2: Set Expectations
```
"I'm excited to dive deep into [product/business] today.

Here's what I'd like to cover:
- First, I want to understand your customers at a deep level
- Then, we'll talk about what makes your approach unique
- We'll cover your story and some success stories
- And we'll touch on the offer and any objections

This helps me write copy that really resonates.

I'd also like to record this call so I can reference it later -
is that okay with you?"
```
*[Get explicit verbal permission for recording]*

#### Step 3: Start Recording
- Start primary recording
- Confirm recording is active
- Start backup recording if available

---

### SECTION 1: CUSTOMER DEEP-DIVE (20-25 minutes)

#### Step 4: Ideal Customer Profile
```
"Let's start with your customers. Describe your ideal customer for me -
not just demographics, but who they really are as a person."
```

**Follow-up probes:**
- "If I walked into a room of your best customers, what would I notice?"
- "What's the #1 thing that makes someone perfect for this?"
- "Who is this NOT for?"

**Note-taking focus:** Demographics, psychographics, identifying characteristics

#### Step 5: Pain Points Extraction
```
"What's the main problem they're trying to solve when they come to you?"
```

**Follow-up probes:**
- "What are they frustrated about?"
- "What have they tried that didn't work?"
- "When someone reaches out, what do they typically say?"
- "What exact words do they use to describe their problem?"
- "What keeps them up at night?"
- "What's their 'rock bottom' moment look like?"

**Note-taking focus:** EXACT PHRASES - write down verbatim what they say

#### Step 6: Dream Outcomes
```
"When someone achieves success with [product], what does that look like?"
```

**Follow-up probes:**
- "Beyond the obvious result, what does this mean for their life?"
- "What would they be able to do that they can't do now?"
- "How would they feel differently?"
- "What words do they use to describe what they want?"

**Note-taking focus:** Emotional outcomes, lifestyle changes, specific results

#### Step 7: Failed Solutions
```
"What have your customers typically tried before finding you?"
```

**Follow-up probes:**
- "Why didn't [those solutions] work?"
- "What do people usually try first?"
- "What's the common wisdom that doesn't work?"

**Note-taking focus:** Specific alternatives, why they fail, frustration points

---

### SECTION 2: UNIQUE MECHANISM (20-25 minutes)

#### Step 8: Differentiation Discovery
```
"Now I want to understand what makes YOUR approach different.
If you had to explain why your solution works when others don't,
what would you say?"
```

**Follow-up probes:**
- "What's the core insight everything is built on?"
- "What do you understand that others seem to miss?"
- "Is there a name for your methodology or system?"
- "What's the counter-intuitive thing you've discovered?"

**Note-taking focus:** The unique angle, the breakthrough, the "secret"

#### Step 9: How It Works
```
"Walk me through how this actually works for someone.
What's the process or journey?"
```

**Follow-up probes:**
- "What are the key steps or phases?"
- "Why does doing it THIS way produce results?"
- "Can you describe this visually or in 3 steps?"
- "What's the science or logic behind it?"

**Note-taking focus:** Process, methodology, logical framework

#### Step 10: The "Aha" Moment
```
"What's the 'aha' moment your customers have?
When does the light bulb go on for them?"
```

**Follow-up probes:**
- "What do they say when they finally 'get it'?"
- "What's the shift that happens in their thinking?"

**Note-taking focus:** The transformation moment, the paradigm shift

---

### SECTION 3: PROOF & STORY (15-20 minutes)

#### Step 11: Customer Success Stories
```
"Tell me about a customer who had a great result.
What was their situation, and what happened?"
```

**Follow-up probes:**
- "What specific results did they achieve?"
- "What was their situation before?"
- "What changed for them?"
- "Do you have 2-3 other stories like this?"

**Note-taking focus:** Specific stories with details, before/after, metrics

#### Step 12: Founder/Origin Story
```
"How did you get into this? What's your story?"
```

**Follow-up probes:**
- "Why do you do this? What's your personal connection?"
- "Was there a moment that changed everything?"
- "How did [product] come to be?"
- "What were you doing before?"

**Note-taking focus:** Personal journey, pivotal moments, motivation

#### Step 13: Credentials & Proof
```
"What makes you the right person to offer this?
What's your background or credentials?"
```

**Follow-up probes:**
- "How many people have you helped?"
- "Any media mentions or features?"
- "Any unique proof that competitors can't claim?"

**Note-taking focus:** Authority builders, credibility elements

---

### SECTION 4: OFFER & OBJECTIONS (10-15 minutes)

#### Step 14: Common Objections
```
"What's the #1 reason someone doesn't buy?"
```

**Follow-up probes:**
- "What objections come up most on sales calls?"
- "What makes people hesitate even when interested?"
- "What's the biggest misconception?"
- "What questions do people ask before buying?"

**Note-taking focus:** Every objection mentioned - these become copy sections

#### Step 15: Pricing Justification
```
"Why is it priced at [price]? What makes it worth that?"
```

**Follow-up probes:**
- "What would someone pay to get similar results elsewhere?"
- "What's the cost of NOT solving this problem?"

**Note-taking focus:** Value justification, cost of inaction

#### Step 16: Guarantee & Risk
```
"What's your guarantee? Why that specific guarantee?"
```

**Follow-up probes:**
- "What's your refund rate?"
- "Why should someone trust this will work for them?"

**Note-taking focus:** Risk reversal elements, confidence signals

---

### SECTION 5: WRAP-UP (5-10 minutes)

#### Step 17: Final Questions
```
"What haven't I asked that I should have?"
```

```
"Is there anything else that would help me write better copy for this?"
```

```
"What would you tell your best friend about this if they needed it?"
```

**Note-taking focus:** Often get gold here - don't rush

#### Step 18: Close the Call
```
"This has been incredibly helpful. I've got tons of great material to work with.

Here's what happens next:
- I'll process this call and pull out key insights
- I'll start the research phase to validate and expand what we discussed
- [Timeline for next milestone]

Any questions before we wrap up?"
```

#### Step 19: Final Admin
- Thank them for their time
- Confirm any follow-up items
- Stop recording
- Confirm recording saved successfully

## During-Call Best Practices

### Listening Techniques
| Technique | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **80/20 Rule** | Listen 80% of the time, talk 20% |
| **Silence is Gold** | Don't fill pauses - let them think |
| **Mirror Back** | Repeat their words: "So you're saying..." |
| **Go Deeper** | "Tell me more about that" |
| **Get Specific** | "Can you give me an example?" |
| **Capture Exact Words** | "What exact words do they use?" |

### Note-Taking Priority
1. **Exact quotes** (most valuable)
2. **Specific stories** (with details)
3. **Unique insights** (things you didn't know)
4. **Emotional moments** (where they got animated)
5. **Unanswered questions** (for research)

### Red Flags to Address
| Red Flag | Response |
|----------|----------|
| Generic answers | Probe for specifics |
| "I don't know" | Ask about specific customers |
| Going off-topic | Redirect gently |
| Low energy | Ask about success stories |
| Overly rehearsed | Ask unexpected follow-ups |

---

## Question Adaptation Guidelines

### If Customer Understanding is Weak
- Ask about specific recent customers by name
- Ask about sales conversations
- Ask about support tickets/complaints
- Ask about refund requests (reveals problems)

### If Mechanism Isn't Clear
- Ask "Why does THIS work when other things don't?"
- Ask about the discovery/creation process
- Ask what they do differently than competitors
- Ask what their best customers say makes the difference

### If Stories Are Generic
- Ask for specific names and situations
- Ask for before/after with numbers
- Ask about the customer's emotional journey
- Ask what surprised them about the result

## Common Issues & Solutions

| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| Client gives short answers | Use follow-up probes; sit in silence; ask for examples |
| Client doesn't know their customer well | Ask about specific recent customers by name |
| Client goes off-topic | "That's great - let me note that. Now, going back to..." |
| Running out of time | Prioritize must-ask questions; schedule follow-up |
| Client seems uncomfortable | Switch to easier questions; build more rapport |
| Recording fails | Take detailed notes; request follow-up if critical |
