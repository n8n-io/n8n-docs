# SOP 2.3: Post-Call Processing

## Purpose
Extract, organize, and synthesize all valuable insights from the kick-off call into actionable documents for the copy project.

---

## Inputs Required

| Input | Source | Required |
|-------|--------|----------|
| Call recording | Task 2.2 | Yes |
| Cleaned transcript | Task 2.2 | Yes |
| Live notes | Task 2.2 | Yes |
| Brain dump | Task 2.2 | Yes |

---

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### Step 1: Full Transcript Review
**[TIME: 30-60 minutes]**

Read through the entire cleaned transcript with a highlighter mindset:

#### Highlight Categories (Use Different Colors/Tags)
| Category | Color/Tag | What to Look For |
|----------|-----------|------------------|
| Pain Points | Red | Problems, frustrations, challenges |
| Desires | Green | Goals, dreams, desired outcomes |
| Exact Phrases | Yellow | Verbatim customer language |
| Stories | Blue | Customer stories, anecdotes |
| Mechanism | Purple | What makes it work, methodology |
| Proof | Orange | Results, credentials, evidence |
| Objections | Pink | Concerns, hesitations, questions |

#### While Reviewing, Note:
- Timestamp of each valuable section
- Type of insight
- Exact quote (verbatim)
- Your interpretation/application

## AI Agent Instructions

### Agent: Insight Extractor

**Trigger:** Transcript available

**Instructions:**
```
You are an Insight Extraction Agent for copywriting projects. Your job is to
analyze interview transcripts and extract copy-relevant insights.

When given a call transcript:

1. EXTRACT all pain points:
   - Identify every problem, frustration, challenge mentioned
   - Capture EXACT phrases used
   - Rate intensity (High/Medium/Low)
   - Note context and frequency

2. EXTRACT all desires:
   - Identify every goal, dream, desired outcome
   - Capture EXACT phrases used
   - Note emotional vs. logical desires
   - Identify deeper meanings

3. EXTRACT all stories:
   - Customer transformation stories
   - Origin/founder stories
   - Anecdotes and examples
   - Note the story arc (before → turning point → after)

4. EXTRACT mechanism insights:
   - What makes this solution different
   - Why other solutions fail
   - The core methodology or approach
   - Any named frameworks or systems

5. EXTRACT proof elements:
   - Specific results mentioned
   - Credentials and authority
   - Data and statistics
   - Testimonial quotes

6. EXTRACT objections:
   - Every concern or hesitation mentioned
   - Common questions
   - Misconceptions

7. COMPILE into structured documents:
   - Voice of Customer document
   - Mechanism Insights document
   - Story Assets document
   - Updated Proof Inventory
   - Insights Summary

OUTPUT:
All documents in specified templates (markdown format)

CRITICAL REQUIREMENTS:
- EXACT quotes are essential - do not paraphrase
- Include timestamps for all quotes
- Note intensity/strength of each element
- Identify gaps where research is needed
- Every pain point and desire needs at least one exact quote
```

### Agent: VOC Compiler

**Trigger:** Insight extraction complete

**Instructions:**
```
You are a Voice of Customer Compilation Agent. Your job is to organize
customer language into a usable reference document.

When given extracted insights:

1. ORGANIZE pain points by:
   - Intensity (strongest first)
   - Frequency (most mentioned first)
   - Emotional vs. logical

2. ORGANIZE desires by:
   - Importance to customer
   - Emotional vs. logical
   - Surface vs. deeper desires

3. CREATE quote bank with:
   - Headlines/hooks potential
   - Pain section quotes
   - Desire section quotes
   - Proof/testimonial quotes
   - Story quotes

4. TAG each quote for:
   - Copy section it fits
   - Strength (1-5)
   - Usage notes

5. IDENTIFY patterns:
   - Repeated phrases
   - Common themes
   - Emotional triggers

OUTPUT:
Complete Voice of Customer document with organized, tagged quotes
```

## Common Issues & Solutions

| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| Transcript missing key sections | Go back to recording for those sections |
| Quotes are paraphrased, not exact | Listen to recording for exact wording |
| Mechanism isn't clear | Note as gap; prioritize in research |
| Too many insights to organize | Focus on top 10 in each category |
| Client gave generic answers | Research will need to fill gaps |
