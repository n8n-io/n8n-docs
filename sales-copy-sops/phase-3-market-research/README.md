# Phase 3: Market Research

## Overview

This phase is where the magic happens. Deep market research uncovers the exact language, pain points, stories, and insights that make copy resonate deeply with the target audience. The goal is to find what people actually say, think, and feel - not what we assume they say, think, and feel.

## Phase Objectives

1. Validate pain points and desires from kick-off call
2. Expand voice of customer with exact phrases and language
3. Find real stories, anecdotes, and examples
4. Identify gaps in competitor offerings
5. Gather proof and supporting evidence
6. Discover unique angles and opportunities

## Research Philosophy

### What We're Looking For

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                  THE RESEARCH GOLDMINE                       │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                                              │
│  1. EXACT PAIN POINTS                                        │
│     └─ What specific problems do they describe?              │
│     └─ What words do they use?                               │
│     └─ How intense is the pain?                              │
│                                                              │
│  2. EXACT PHRASES & LANGUAGE                                 │
│     └─ How do they describe the problem?                     │
│     └─ How do they describe what they want?                  │
│     └─ What metaphors and analogies do they use?             │
│                                                              │
│  3. DREAM OUTCOMES                                           │
│     └─ What does success look like to them?                  │
│     └─ What would change in their life?                      │
│     └─ How do they describe the ideal scenario?              │
│                                                              │
│  4. FAILED SOLUTIONS                                         │
│     └─ What have they tried that didn't work?                │
│     └─ WHY didn't it work?                                   │
│     └─ What are they frustrated with?                        │
│                                                              │
│  5. UNIQUE MECHANISM CLUES                                   │
│     └─ What underlying problem do they not see?              │
│     └─ What makes some solutions work and others fail?       │
│     └─ What's the real obstacle?                             │
│                                                              │
│  6. PROOF & EVIDENCE                                         │
│     └─ Success stories and transformations                   │
│     └─ Data and statistics                                   │
│     └─ Expert opinions                                       │
│                                                              │
│  7. STORIES & ANECDOTES                                      │
│     └─ Interesting examples and cases                        │
│     └─ Historical parallels                                  │
│     └─ Relatable situations                                  │
│                                                              │
│  8. MARKET GAPS                                              │
│     └─ What are competitors missing?                         │
│     └─ What do customers wish they had?                      │
│     └─ Bonus/add-on opportunities                            │
│                                                              │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### The Golden Rule of Research

> **Capture EXACT quotes. Never paraphrase.**

The difference between "people are frustrated" and "I've been at this for 3 years and I'm so frustrated I could scream" is the difference between mediocre and magnetic copy.

## Research Best Practices

### Do's
- Capture exact quotes verbatim
- Note the source/link for each quote
- Look for patterns across sources
- Pay attention to emotional intensity
- Save interesting but tangential finds
- Research until you see repeating patterns

### Don'ts
- Don't paraphrase - use exact words
- Don't cherry-pick - capture the full picture
- Don't stop too early - dig deep
- Don't ignore negative/skeptical views
- Don't assume - let the research show you

---

## Research Stopping Criteria

You've done enough research when:

1. **Patterns emerge** - You see the same themes repeatedly
2. **Language saturates** - You keep seeing the same phrases
3. **Stories repeat** - Similar situations keep appearing
4. **Questions answered** - Your research questions have answers
5. **Confidence high** - You feel you understand the market
