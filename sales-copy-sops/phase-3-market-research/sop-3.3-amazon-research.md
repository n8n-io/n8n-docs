# SOP 3.3: Amazon Review Research

## Purpose
Extract customer language, expectations, disappointments, and market gaps from Amazon reviews of competitive or related products.

---

## Why Amazon Reviews?

Amazon reviews are uniquely valuable because:
- Customers share honest opinions (verified purchases)
- Reviews reveal expectations vs. reality
- Negative reviews expose gaps to fill
- Positive reviews show what resonates
- Language is authentic and emotional
- Before/after stories appear naturally
- Objections are addressed by other reviewers

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### Step 1: Identify Products to Research
**[TIME: 15-20 minutes]**

#### Product Types to Find

**Direct Competitors:**
- Products that solve the same problem
- Products at similar price points
- Market leaders in the space

**Related Products:**
- Products solving related problems
- Products for the same audience
- Complementary products

**Books in the Space:**
- How-to books on the topic
- Expert/guru books
- Self-help books in the category

### Step 2: Review Analysis Strategy
**[TIME: 3-4 hours total for analysis]**

For each product, analyze reviews systematically:

#### Star Rating Strategy
| Rating | What to Look For | Time Allocation |
|--------|------------------|-----------------|
| 5-star | What they love; exceeded expectations | 20% |
| 4-star | Praise with minor critiques | 15% |
| 3-star | Balanced views; specific issues | 25% |
| 2-star | Significant disappointments | 20% |
| 1-star | Complete failures; deal-breakers | 20% |

#### Review Filters to Use
- **Verified Purchase Only** - More credible
- **Most Recent** - Current relevance
- **Most Helpful** - Community-validated insights

### Step 3: Extract from 5-Star Reviews
**[TIME: 30-45 min per product]**

Look for:
- What exceeded expectations
- Specific results mentioned
- Emotional language about outcomes
- What made them finally buy
- Who they'd recommend it to
- Before/after descriptions

#### Extraction Template (5-Star)
```markdown
## 5-Star Review Extract

**Product:** [Name]
**Review Date:** [Date]
**Verified:** [Yes/No]

**Quote:** "[Exact quote - the best parts]"

**What They Loved:**
- [Specific thing 1]
- [Specific thing 2]

**Results Mentioned:** [Specific outcomes]
**Emotional Language:** "[Phrases to note]"
**Copy Application:** [How to use]
```

### Step 4: Extract from 3-Star Reviews
**[TIME: 45-60 min per product]**

3-star reviews are often the most valuable - balanced, specific, and honest.

Look for:
- What worked vs. what didn't
- Specific criticisms
- Who it worked for vs. who it didn't
- Expectations vs. reality
- Suggestions for improvement

#### Extraction Template (3-Star)
```markdown
## 3-Star Review Extract

**Product:** [Name]
**Review Date:** [Date]
**Verified:** [Yes/No]

**Quote:** "[Exact quote]"

**What Worked:** [Specific positives]
**What Didn't Work:** [Specific negatives]
**Gap Identified:** [What's missing]
**Copy Application:** [How to address/differentiate]
```

### Step 5: Extract from 1-2 Star Reviews
**[TIME: 45-60 min per product]**

These reveal gaps, objections, and opportunities.

Look for:
- Deal-breakers
- Unmet expectations
- Missing features/elements
- Who it didn't work for
- Complaints about experience
- Comparisons to other products

#### Extraction Template (1-2 Star)
```markdown

**Review Date:** [Date]
**Verified:** [Yes/No]
```

## Advanced Amazon Techniques

### Technique 1: Review Mining by Keyword
In the reviews section, search for:
- "wish" (reveals gaps)
- "disappointed" (reveals failures)
- "expected" (reveals expectations)
- "finally" (reveals journey)
- "tried everything" (reveals failed solutions)
- "compared to" (reveals competition)

### Technique 2: Review Images/Videos
Customer photos and videos show:
- Real-world use
- Before/after results
- Quality expectations
- Unboxing experience

### Technique 3: "Top Reviews" Analysis
Amazon's "Top Reviews" are community-validated. Pay extra attention to these.

### Technique 4: Review Velocity
Look at recent reviews separately from all-time. Product may have changed.

### Technique 5: Reviewer Profile Mining
Click on helpful reviewers to see:
- Other products they've reviewed
- Their expertise level
- Purchasing patterns
