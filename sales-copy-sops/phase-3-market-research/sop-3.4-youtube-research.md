# SOP 3.4: YouTube Comments Research

## Purpose
Extract insights, questions, and language from YouTube video comments where engaged audiences discuss topics related to your product's market.

---

## Why YouTube Comments?

YouTube comments are valuable because:
- Commenters are engaged (took time to watch and comment)
- Questions reveal specific concerns and gaps
- Discussions show points of confusion
- Success stories appear naturally
- Objections surface in discussions
- Real-time reactions to content reveal resonance

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### Step 1: Identify Target Videos
**[TIME: 20-30 minutes]**

#### Video Types to Find

**Educational Videos:**
- "How to [achieve goal]"
- "[Topic] tutorial"
- "[Topic] explained"
- "[Topic] guide"

**Review/Comparison Videos:**
- "[Product type] review"
- "[Product A] vs [Product B]"
- "Best [product type] 2024"
- "Is [product] worth it?"

**Story/Transformation Videos:**
- "[Topic] journey"
- "My [topic] story"
- "How I [achieved result]"
- "Before and after [topic]"

**Expert/Authority Videos:**
- Videos from known experts
- Podcast clips on topic
- Interview videos
- Conference talks

#### Search Strategy
```
YouTube Search: "[topic keyword]"
Filter by: Upload date (Last year), View count

YouTube Search: "[topic] how to"
YouTube Search: "[topic] transformation"
YouTube Search: "[product type] review"
YouTube Search: "[competitor name]"
```

### Step 2: Video Selection Criteria
**[TIME: 10 minutes]**

Prioritize videos with:
| Criteria | Why It Matters |
|----------|----------------|
| 10K+ views | Indicates relevance |
| 100+ comments | Rich discussion |
| Recent (1-2 years) | Current relevance |
| High engagement ratio | Active audience |
| Relevant content | On-topic for your market |

### Step 3: Comment Analysis
**[TIME: 2-3 hours]**

For each video, analyze comments systematically:

#### Comment Sorting Strategy
1. **Top Comments** - Most resonant with community
2. **Newest** - Recent concerns and questions
3. **Creator Replies** - What questions get answered

#### What to Look For
| Type | Examples | Note This |
|------|----------|-----------|
| Questions | "How do you...?" | Knowledge gaps |
| Results shared | "I did this and..." | Proof/stories |
| Objections | "But doesn't this...?" | Concerns |
| Praise | "This changed..." | What resonates |
| Criticism | "This doesn't work because..." | Objections |
| Requests | "Can you make a video on...?" | Gaps |

## Advanced YouTube Techniques

### Technique 1: Comment Sorting Tools
- **TubeBuddy** - Better comment filtering
- **vidIQ** - Comment analytics
- **YouTube Comment Search** - Chrome extension

### Technique 2: Timestamp Comments
Look for comments like "Great point at 5:32" - these indicate key moments.

### Technique 3: Competitor Comment Analysis
Analyze comments on competitor videos for:
- Questions about their product
- Complaints about their approach
- Praise you need to match

### Technique 4: Live Chat Replays
If video was livestream, chat replay contains:
- Real-time reactions
- Spontaneous questions
- Immediate responses

### Technique 5: Community Tab Posts
Check channel community tabs for:
- Polls (reveal preferences)
- Engagement posts
- Question threads

## AI Agent Instructions

### Agent: YouTube Comment Agent

**Trigger:** Video list created

**Instructions:**
```
You are a YouTube Comment Analysis Agent for copywriting projects. Your job is
to extract valuable insights from YouTube video comments.

When given a list of videos:

1. IDENTIFY high-value comments:
   - High like counts (community validated)
   - Questions (reveal gaps)
   - Stories (proof and examples)
   - Objections (concerns to address)
   - Requests (opportunities)

2. EXTRACT comments verbatim:
   - Include like count
   - Note video source
   - Capture reply threads if relevant

3. CATEGORIZE by type:
   - Questions
   - Success stories
   - Objections
   - Pain points
   - Desires
   - Content requests

4. IDENTIFY patterns:
   - Common questions
   - Recurring concerns
   - Shared experiences

5. COMPILE into YouTube Research Document

OUTPUT:
Complete YouTube Research Document (markdown format)

CRITICAL REQUIREMENTS:
- All comments must be exact/verbatim
- Include like counts for credibility
- Note video source for each comment
- Identify patterns across videos
- Create question bank from findings
```
