# SOP 6.1: Long-Form Sales Copy (SLA Framework)

## Purpose
Write complete long-form sales copy (sales pages, VSLs, webinars) using the SLA Framework: Story → Logic → Action.

## Section-by-Section Writing Guide

### SECTION 1: HEADLINE

**Elements Required:**
- New-ness/freshness
- Curiosity/unexpected connection
- Benefit
- Authority/credibility tease

**Formula Options:**

```
1. How To [Achieve Desired Outcome] Without [Common Obstacle]...

2. The [Adjective] [Mechanism Name] That [Benefit] in [Timeframe]...

3. [Authority Figure] Reveals [Surprising Truth] About [Topic]...

4. Warning: [Common Approach] May Be [Negative Consequence]
   (Here's What [Expert/We] Discovered Instead...)

5. [Number] [Adjective] Ways to [Benefit]
   (Even If [Common Objection])...
```

**Subheadline:**
Expand on the headline promise or add credibility/curiosity.

**Writing Checklist:**
- [ ] Contains benefit (what they get)
- [ ] Contains curiosity element (makes them want to know more)
- [ ] Contains newness/freshness (not the same old thing)
- [ ] Hints at authority/credibility
- [ ] Uses language from VOC when possible

---

### SECTION 2: STORY OPENING

**Purpose:** Hook them emotionally with a relatable story.

**Story Structure:**

```
1. HOOK (First 1-3 sentences)
   - Start in the middle of action/emotion
   - Create immediate curiosity
   - Example: "The email came at 3:47 AM..."

2. CHARACTER (Who the story is about)
   - Someone reader can relate to INTERNALLY
   - Establish their situation quickly
   - Show their struggle/problem

3. CONFLICT (The problem/challenge)
   - Paint the pain vividly
   - Use specific details
   - Show what was at stake

4. GLIMPSE OF HOPE (Transition)
   - Hint that a solution exists
   - But don't reveal it yet
   - Create curiosity for what comes next
```

**Story Sources:**
- Client's story
- Customer story
- Historical anecdote
- Pop culture reference (with compliance)
- Personal observation

**Writing Tips:**
- Use present tense for immediacy
- Include sensory details
- Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences)
- End with a hook to the next section

---

### SECTION 3: PUZZLING QUESTION

**Purpose:** Verbalize the curiosity the story created.

**Formula:**
```
"How is it possible that [Person A with disadvantage]
achieved [Amazing Result] while [Person B with advantages]
couldn't [achieve even basic result]?"

"That's the biggest question we'll try to answer today..."
```

**Transition to credibility:**
```
"And to answer it, I have to introduce you to [expert/self]..."

OR

"Before I reveal the answer, you need to understand something
that 99% of people get wrong about [topic]..."
```

---

### SECTION 4: CREDIBILITY ESTABLISHMENT

**Purpose:** Answer "Why should I listen to you?"

**Elements to Include:**
- Who you/the expert is
- Relevant credentials
- Experience with this problem
- Results achieved (preview)
- Why you care about helping

**Structure:**
```
1. Brief introduction
2. Relevant background/credentials
3. "I've helped X people achieve Y"
4. Personal connection to the problem
5. Transition to alternatives section
```

**Tone:** Confident but not arrogant. Helpful, not boastful.

---

### SECTION 5-6: ALTERNATIVES & CRACKS

**Purpose:** Show why common solutions don't work (setup for mechanism).

**Alternative Solution Section:**
```
"When most people struggle with [problem], they usually try:

Solution #1: [Name]
[What it is and why it seems logical]

Solution #2: [Name]
[What it is and why people try it]

Solution #3: [Name]
[What it is and why it's common advice]
```

**Cracks Section:**
```
"But here's what most people don't realize about [Solution #1]...

[Stat, story, or logic showing why it fails]

The truth is, [Solution #1] fails because [reason].

And [Solution #2]? Even worse...

[Evidence showing the flaw]
```

**Key:** Don't attack the solutions. Show their limitations with compassion.

---

### SECTION 7: UNIQUE MECHANISM

**Purpose:** Reveal why YOUR solution works when others don't.

**Structure:**
```
1. TRANSITION
   "So if [solutions] don't work, what does?"

2. THE INSIGHT
   "Here's what I discovered: [Counter-intuitive truth]"

3. THE MECHANISM (Named)
   "I call it the [Mechanism Name]..."

4. HOW IT WORKS (3-5 steps)
   "Here's how it works:

   Step 1: [Name]
   [What happens and why it matters]

   Step 2: [Name]
   [What happens and why it matters]

   Step 3: [Name]
   [What happens and why it matters]"

5. WHY IT WORKS
   "This works because [logic/science]..."

6. VISUAL (if applicable)
   [Diagram or visual representation]

7. PROOF
   "Don't just take my word for it:
   [Evidence, study, testimonial]"
```

---

### SECTION 8: PILLARS/PRINCIPLES/SECRETS

**Purpose:** Break down the mechanism into digestible pieces.

**Structure for Each Pillar:**
```
## Pillar 1: [Name]

[Claim/headline for this pillar]

[Story or example illustrating this]

Here's why this matters:
[Explanation of importance]

How to do it:
- [Tip 1]
- [Tip 2]
- [Tip 3]

[Testimonial or proof - optional]

And that leads us to the next critical piece...
```

**Repeat for each pillar (typically 3-5)**

---

### SECTION 9: OBJECTION HANDLING

**Purpose:** Address remaining doubts before the pitch.

**Common Objections to Address:**
- "Will this work for me?" (specificity)
- "I've tried everything" (differentiation)
- "Is this legit?" (proof)
- "Why haven't I heard of this?" (newness)
- "Is it too good to be true?" (realistic expectations)

**Format:**
```
"Now, you might be thinking: '[Objection]'

And I completely understand why you'd feel that way.

Here's the thing:
[Address the objection with logic, proof, or story]

[Evidence supporting your response]"
```

---

### SECTION 10: TRANSITION TO PITCH

**Purpose:** Smoothly move from education to offer.

**Structure:**
```
"So here's what I did:

After [discovering/developing] this, I knew I had to [systematize/share] it.

I couldn't keep this to myself when I knew how many people were struggling with [problem].

So I put together everything I know into [Product Name]..."
```

---

### SECTION 11: RESULTS/TESTIMONIALS

**Purpose:** Show proof that it works for real people.

**Structure:**
```
1. Return to opening story (if applicable)
   "[Person from intro] used this exact approach to [result]"

2. Diverse testimonials showing:
   - Different starting points
   - Different backgrounds
   - Specific results
   - Emotional transformations

3. Data/statistics (if available)
```

**Testimonial Format:**
- Quote with specific result
- Who they are (relatability)
- Before state → After state
- How long it took

---

### SECTION 12: THE CROSSROADS

**Purpose:** Make them feel the weight of the decision.

**The Three Paths:**
```
"Right now, you have three choices:

**Choice #1: Do nothing**
[Paint picture of continued struggle]
[Use their pain language]
[Show the cost of inaction]

**Choice #2: Try to figure it out yourself**
[Paint picture of slow, uncertain progress]
[Show the cost in time and frustration]

**Choice #3: Join [Product/Program Name]**
[Paint picture of supported success]
[Show the transformation ahead]

The choice is yours..."
```

---

### SECTION 13: THE OFFER

**Use the Offer Blueprint from Phase 5**

**Presentation Order:**
1. Introduce the product
2. Show what's included (core)
3. Assign value to core
4. Introduce Bonus 1 + value
5. Introduce Bonus 2 + value
6. Introduce Bonus 3 + value
7. Stack the total value
8. Reveal the price (anchored)
9. Present the guarantee
10. Add urgency/scarcity (if genuine)
11. CTA

---

### SECTION 14: FAQ

**Purpose:** Handle final objections and provide clarity.

**Questions to Include:**
- "Is this right for me if...?"
- "How long until I see results?"
- "What if it doesn't work?"
- "How is this different from...?"
- "What do I get exactly?"
- "How does the guarantee work?"

---

### SECTION 15-16: FINAL CTA & P.S.

**Final CTA:**
```
"[Transformation statement]

[Reminder of risk reversal]

[Clear action step]

[Button/CTA]"
```

**P.S. Sections:**
- P.S. #1: Summarize the offer
- P.P.S. #2: Guarantee reminder
- P.P.P.S. #3: Urgency or social proof
